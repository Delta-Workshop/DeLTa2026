Design Deep Learning with Principles: From Theory to Applications

Home | Topics | Speakers | Call for Papers | Organizers

Description

Deep learning (DL) has significantly advanced artificial intelligence (AI) in the past decade. This workshop focuses on proactively using deep learning theory to design principled practices and applications, specifically aiming to unify and promote a clear research direction: How to design principles using falsifiable predictions of the properties of deep learning systems? Deep learning theory aims to explain neural networks (perfect memorization [1], extrapolating generalization [2], benefits of depth [3], etc.), with carefully crafted assumptions and conditions. However, current theories fall short of establishing sustainable and practical principles: 1) Significant gaps between assumptions and practices, being unable to reliably describe the real world; 2) Rapidly evolving AI paradigms (data-centric AI [4], foundation models [5], pre-training/fine-tuning, prompting [6], etc.) quickly redefine research focuses of theory works.
